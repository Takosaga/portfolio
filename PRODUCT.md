# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Employers and technical recruiters scanning for AI/ML talent. They skim quickly, looking for credible signals: concrete projects, specific tech stack depth, career trajectory, and proof of shipping real systems — not just tutorials or notebooks.

## Product Purpose
A personal portfolio that converts a brief scan into a strong hire/interview callback. It demonstrates technical competence through shipped projects, shows career progression from teaching to AI engineering, and provides clear pathways to contact (email, GitHub, LinkedIn). The embedded token-usage dashboard is a proof-of-work artifact — it's a real system I built and maintain.

## Brand Personality
**Sharp — Technical — Confident.** Engineering-forward voice: precise language, no fluff, minimal warmth. Think clean terminal aesthetics, data-driven presentation, quiet confidence. The copy reads like a well-documented API — every word earns its place. The personality reflects someone who builds systems that work, not someone who talks about building them.

## Anti-references
- Not dark-mode-by-default because it looks "techy." Light theme is the starting point.
- Not SaaS-cream: no generic white/blue corporate startup template, no hero metric cards with gradient accents.
- No identical project card grids with icons and short descriptions repeated mindlessly.

## Design Principles
1. **Show, don't tell.** Let projects, data, and shipped systems speak. Every section should present evidence, not claims.
2. **Precision over decoration.** Every visual element serves a purpose — information, navigation, or interaction. Nothing exists just to look nice.
3. **Engineering clarity.** Clean structure, readable typography, logical hierarchy. The interface should feel like well-organized code: intentional, consistent, no surprises.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG AA baseline. Skip-to-content link, proper ARIA attributes, keyboard navigation for lightbox and mobile nav, focus-visible outlines, reduced-motion support already in place. Color choices must maintain sufficient contrast ratios against the background.
